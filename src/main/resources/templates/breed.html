Horse Breeds

Light Horse Breeds

Arabian
Origin: Middle East

One of the oldest breeds, known for endurance, intelligence, and distinctive dished face. Typically 14.1-15.1 hands.

  • Uses: Endurance, show, pleasure
  • Colors: Bay, gray, chestnut, black
Thoroughbred
Origin: England

Bred for racing, known for speed and spirit. Typically 15-17 hands. Hot-blooded temperament.

  • Uses: Racing, eventing, show jumping
  • Colors: Bay, brown, chestnut, gray, black
American Quarter Horse
Origin: United States

Muscular, versatile breed excelling at sprinting short distances. Typically 14.3-16 hands.

  • Uses: Racing, ranch work, rodeo
  • Colors: All colors, most common sorrel
Appaloosa
Origin: United States

Known for colorful spotted coat patterns. Versatile with strong endurance. Typically 14.2-16 hands.

  • Uses: Trail, pleasure, ranch work
  • Colors: Spotted patterns (leopard, blanket, snowflake)

Draft Horse Breeds

Clydesdale
Origin: Scotland

Large, powerful draft horse with distinctive feathering on legs. Typically 16-18 hands.

  • Uses: Farm work, driving, show
  • Colors: Bay with white markings, roan
Percheron
Origin: France

Elegant draft breed known for intelligence and willingness to work. Typically 15-19 hands.

  • Uses: Farming, logging, driving
  • Colors: Gray, black
Shire
Origin: England

One of the largest horse breeds, known for great strength. Typically 16-17.2 hands.

  • Uses: Heavy farm work, brewing industry
  • Colors: Black, bay, gray
Belgian Draft
Origin: Belgium

Powerful draft horse with a gentle disposition. Typically 16-17 hands.

  • Uses: Farming, logging, show
  • Colors: Chestnut with flaxen mane/tail

Pony Breeds

Shetland Pony
Origin: Shetland Islands

Small but very strong pony breed. Typically under 10.2 hands.

  • Uses: Children's mount, driving
  • Colors: All colors except spotted
Welsh Pony
Origin: Wales

Elegant pony with excellent movement and temperament. Sections A-D by size.

  • Uses: Riding, driving, jumping
  • Colors: All colors except piebald/skewbald
Connemara
Origin: Ireland

Largest of the pony breeds, known for athleticism. Typically 13-15 hands.

  • Uses: Sport pony, jumping, eventing
  • Colors: Gray, bay, brown, black
Hackney Pony
Origin: England

Known for high-stepping action and elegance. Typically 12-14 hands.

  • Uses: Driving, show
  • Colors: Bay, brown, chestnut, black

Sport Horse Breeds

Hanoverian
Origin: Germany

Premier warmblood breed excelling in dressage and jumping. Typically 15.3-17.2 hands.

  • Uses: Dressage, show jumping, eventing
  • Colors: Bay, chestnut, black, gray
Dutch Warmblood
Origin: Netherlands

Athletic sport horse with excellent jumping ability. Typically 16-17 hands.

  • Uses: Show jumping, dressage, driving
  • Colors: All solid colors
Trakehner
Origin: East Prussia

Elegant warmblood known for floating trot and athleticism. Typically 15.2-17 hands.

  • Uses: Dressage, eventing, combined driving
  • Colors: All solid colors
Oldenburg
Origin: Germany

Powerful yet elegant warmblood with exceptional movement. Typically 16-17.2 hands.

  • Uses: Dressage, show jumping
  • Colors: All colors, often black or bay
